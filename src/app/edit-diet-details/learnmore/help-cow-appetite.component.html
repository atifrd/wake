Knowing how much a cow can eat in a day is important in formulating diets. Predicting feed intake is complex because of the many factors that may affect a dairy cowâ€™s appetite limit. These include:

Animal factors
  • Liveweight
  • Age
  • Stage of lactation
  • Stage of pregnancy
  • Milk yield
  • Body condition
  • Infectious/metabolic disease
Feed factors
  • Dry matter content
  • Energy content
  • Fibre content
  • Nutrient balance
  • Rate of rumen passage
  • Palatability
  • Physical / chemical processing
  • Previous exposure to certain feeds
  • Presence of contaminants
Environmental factors
  • Sequencing of feeds each day
  • Feed trough and feed pad design/space
  • Pasture allocation and density
  • Day length
  • Housing, weather
Water factors
  • Water access
  • Water quality
