How to formulate a diet for a given month, for a given class of stock

Firstly, if you have a pasture in the diet, click on 'ESTIMATE AVAILABILITY'

  • Enter grazing area and select daily growth rate and utilisation rate to determine tonnes Dry Matter available for the month (assuming no change in pasture cover)

Then click on 'FORMULATE DIET'

  • For each feed, enter feeding rate (kgs per head per day consumed on a Dry Matter or as-fed basis, as selected on the Feed screen when you added the feed)
    • For a Pasture, note the Surplus/Deficit value – This compares kgs DM per head per day consumed with tonnes Dry Matter available for the month.
      • If your selected feeding rate results in a Deficit (-ve value), you will need to reduce feeding rate until the deficit is eliminated
      • If your selected feeding rate results in a substantial Surplus (+ve value), you should review your forage plan
    • For all feeds other than pastures and other grazed forages, note that a wastage value must be entered. See the 'LEARN MORE ABOUT WASTAGE' help box.
  • View the 3 horizontal bars in the right hand column. These show % of animal’s daily Metabolisable Energy and Metabolisable Protein requirements provided by the diet, and Feed Intake as a % of the animal’s estimated appetite limit
  • Below these are the diet’s feed efficiency, nutrient specifications (with warnings if diet is not well balanced), cost, income from milk produced and margin over feed cost
